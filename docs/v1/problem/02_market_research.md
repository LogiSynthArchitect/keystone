## 2.1 Target Audience

**Primary Users — Independent Locksmith Technicians in Ghana**

Who they are:
- Self-employed or informally partnered technical service workers
- Operate in urban and peri-urban areas, primarily Greater Accra, Kumasi, Takoradi
- Age range 20-40 years, predominantly male
- Earn income per job, no fixed salary, no formal business registration in most cases
- Android smartphone users — Android accounts for 80.9% of smartphone OS in Ghana
- Active daily WhatsApp users — WhatsApp is the most used social platform in Ghana
- Use Facebook and Instagram for informal advertising of their work
- Have real technical skills but zero business management infrastructure

What they care about most:
- Getting more jobs and repeat customers
- Being seen as professional and trustworthy
- Not losing customer contacts or job details
- Building a reputation that grows their income

Technical proficiency:
- Medium — comfortable with smartphones, WhatsApp, social media
- Not comfortable with complex software or tools requiring setup
- Will abandon any tool that takes more than 2 minutes to understand

Primary device: Android smartphone (Tecno, Itel, Samsung — most common affordable brands in Ghana)

**Secondary Users — Customers of Locksmith Technicians**

Who they are:
- Ghanaian individuals, households, and small businesses in urban areas
- Vehicle owners needing car lock and key programming
- Homeowners needing door lock installation, repair, or smart lock installation
- Small business owners needing security hardware maintenance
- Do NOT interact with the app directly in V1
- Receive value passively through WhatsApp follow-up messages sent by technicians

**Anti-Audience — Who Keystone Is NOT For**
- Large formal security companies with existing enterprise software
- Technicians outside the locksmith and technical services space
- Customers seeking a booking marketplace in V1
- Technicians who are not smartphone users
- Technicians based in rural areas with unreliable mobile data in V1

## 2.2 Market Size Estimation

**Context: Ghana's Informal Economy**

Ghana's informal economy is massive and structurally relevant to Keystone:
- 92.3% of all businesses in Ghana are informal (Ghana Statistical Service, 2024)
- 80% of Ghana's workforce is employed in the informal sector
- The services sector is the largest employer with 5.5 million workers as of 2024
- Artisans, technicians, and skilled tradespeople make up a significant share of informal services

**Mobile Technology Penetration (critical for app viability):**
- 38.95 million mobile connections in Ghana as of January 2024 — 113% of population
- 69.8% internet penetration — 24.06 million internet users
- 70% of mobile connections are broadband (3G to 5G)
- Android is dominant OS at 80.9% of smartphone users
- WhatsApp is the number one social and communication platform in Ghana
- Mobile phones generate over 77% of web traffic in Ghana

**Market Size Reasoning:**

Total Addressable Market (TAM):
- All independent technical service workers in Ghana's informal sector who own smartphones
- Estimated conservatively at 50,000-150,000 technicians across all trades in urban Ghana
- No precise locksmith-specific data exists — this is an underresearched segment

Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM):
- Independent locksmith and security hardware technicians in Greater Accra, Kumasi, and Takoradi
- Estimated at 5,000-15,000 technicians in these three cities
- These are smartphone users with WhatsApp access and sufficient mobile data

Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM) — Year 1:
- Realistic target: 50-200 active technicians using Keystone by end of year one
- Starting with 2 founding technicians, growing through word of mouth and demonstrated results
- Conservative but honest — adoption in informal sectors requires trust built over time

Important note: Precise locksmith industry statistics for Ghana are not publicly available.
These estimates are based on informal sector workforce data, mobile penetration data,
and reasonable extrapolation. Real numbers will emerge through direct market validation
with Jeremie and Jean as first users.

## 2.3 Competitor Analysis

**Competitor 1 — Workiz**
- Type: Direct (field service management for locksmiths specifically)
- Core Offering: Job scheduling, customer management, invoicing, team management
- Strengths: Feature-rich, built specifically for locksmith businesses, mobile app available
- Weaknesses: Priced from $65-$225/month USD, built entirely for Western markets,
  requires reliable internet, assumes formal business structure, English-only,
  payment processing tied to US/EU systems
- Pricing: $65-$225/month USD
- Why Ghanaian technicians will NOT use it: Too expensive, too complex, not built for their context

**Competitor 2 — Jobber**
- Type: Direct (field service management)
- Core Offering: Scheduling, quoting, invoicing, customer follow-up
- Strengths: Clean UI, strong customer follow-up features, good mobile app
- Weaknesses: Starts at $49/month USD, Western-market focus, requires credit card, complex onboarding
- Pricing: $49-$249/month USD
- Why Ghanaian technicians will NOT use it: Cost and context mismatch

**Competitor 3 — Zoho FSM**
- Type: Direct (field service management, has African presence)
- Core Offering: End-to-end field service operations management
- Strengths: More affordable than Workiz/Jobber, has South Africa presence, mobile app
- Weaknesses: Still complex for a solo independent technician, requires formal business setup,
  not designed for informal single-person operations, no WhatsApp integration for Ghana context
- Pricing: Free tier limited, paid from $30/month USD
- Why Ghanaian technicians will NOT use it: Too complex, not designed for solo informal operators

**Competitor 4 — WhatsApp Business (Indirect)**
- Type: Indirect substitute
- Core Offering: Business profile, quick replies, catalog, broadcast messaging
- Strengths: Free, already installed, familiar, works on any Android phone
- Weaknesses: No customer job history, no knowledge base, no structured follow-up automation,
  no job logging, no professional profile beyond basic info
- Why it is not enough: It is a communication tool, not a business management tool

**Competitor 5 — Paper and Phone Contacts (Substitute)**
- Type: Substitute behavior
- Core Offering: Manual record keeping through notebooks and phone contacts
- Strengths: Zero cost, zero learning curve, always available offline
- Weaknesses: Not searchable, lost when phone breaks, no follow-up capability,
  no knowledge preservation
- Why it is not enough: Does not scale and loses value over time

## 2.4 Market Gaps

**Gap 1 — No tool exists for solo informal technicians in Ghana**
Every field service management tool on the market assumes a business with multiple employees,
a manager, an office, and a formal payment system. There is no tool designed for one or two
people working independently from their smartphones in Ghana.

**Gap 2 — No technical knowledge preservation tool exists**
No competitor has a feature specifically designed for technicians to save job-specific technical
solutions, bypass codes, or unusual techniques for future reference. This gap exists globally,
not just in Ghana.

**Gap 3 — No WhatsApp-native follow-up system for African technicians**
WhatsApp is the dominant communication platform in Ghana. No field service tool integrates
meaningfully with WhatsApp for customer follow-up in the African context.

**Gap 4 — No trust-based franchise model for informal technical services**
No platform in Ghana is building a quality-validated network of independent technicians under
a single brand. The market is completely fragmented — every technician is invisible and unverified.

**Gap 5 — Pricing gap**
Existing tools cost $30-$225/month USD. For an independent locksmith in Ghana earning per job,
this is not viable. A tool priced appropriately for the Ghanaian market has no competition.

## 2.6 Regulatory and Compliance Factors

**Data Privacy:**
- Ghana's Data Protection Act 2012 (Act 843) governs personal data collection and processing
- Keystone will collect customer names and phone numbers — this falls under Act 843
- Requirement: Inform users what data is collected and how it is used
- Requirement: Allow users to request deletion of their data
- No GDPR obligation unless European users are targeted — not a V1 concern

**Business Registration:**
- Keystone as a platform will need to be registered as a business in Ghana eventually
- V1 as a tool used by two people does not require formal registration immediately
- As the platform grows and handles payments, registration becomes mandatory

**WhatsApp Business API:**
- Using WhatsApp Business API for automated messages requires Meta Business verification
- For V1 with two users, WhatsApp deep links (wa.me links) can be used without API approval
- As the platform scales, proper WhatsApp Business API integration will require Meta approval

**Payment Regulation:**
- If Keystone charges subscription fees, MTN Mobile Money or similar will apply
- Bank of Ghana regulates digital payments and mobile money
- No payment processing required in V1 — the app is free for founding members
